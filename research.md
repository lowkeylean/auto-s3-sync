# Hotel CMMS & Preventive Maintenance Research

## Executive Summary
The most successful hotel engineering platforms share a focus on **mobility, simplicity, and asset-centric workflows**. They move away from complex, desktop-heavy interfaces to mobile-first apps that empower engineers on the floor.

## Top Performers (The "Successful Platforms")
These platforms are currently dominating the hotel engineering space because they balance powerful features with ease of use.

### 1. Transcendent (by Actabl)
*   **Best For:** Enterprise Asset Management & CapEx Planning.
*   **Why it wins:** It’s not just a "fix-it" tool; it helps owners plan long-term capital expenses (CapEx) based on asset health. It uses QR codes and deep asset data to drive decisions.
*   **Key Differentiator:** The link between daily maintenance and financial planning.

### 2. hotelkit
*   **Best For:** Team Collaboration & Daily Operations.
*   **Why it wins:** It serves as a "digital logbook" for the entire hotel, not just engineering. It breaks down silos between housekeeping, front desk, and engineering.
*   **Key Differentiator:** Social-media-like interface that requires zero training.

### 3. ALICE (now part of Actabl)
*   **Best For:** Guest Experience & Rapid Response.
*   **Why it wins:** It centers maintenance around the *guest*. A broken AC isn't just a work order; it's a guest recovery opportunity.
*   **Key Differentiator:** Seamless integration with Guest Services and Housekeeping.

### 4. Guestware
*   **Best For:** Process Optimization & Quality Control.
*   **Why it wins:** Strong emphasis on "Rapid Response" workflows to ensure guest problems are solved before they escalate.

### 5. UpKeep & Limble CMMS
*   **Best For:** Modern, Mobile-First Maintenance (Cross-industry but popular in hospitality).
*   **Why it wins:** Extremely modern UI/UX. They feel like consumer apps (like Instagram or Uber) rather than clunky enterprise software.

---

## What They All Have in Common
1.  **Mobile-First Design:** The engineer's primary interface is a phone, not a desktop.
2.  **Photo-Centric Workflows:** "A picture is worth a thousand words." Issues and resolutions are documented with photos.
3.  **Simple Adoption:** They require minimal training. If an engineer can use WhatsApp, they can use these tools.
4.  **Cloud-Based:** Real-time data sync across the property.

## The 80/20 Rule: Features That Drive Results
Applying the Pareto Principle (80% of results come from 20% of features), here are the critical features that matter most. If you build these, you capture the vast majority of the value.

### 1. Mobile Work Order Management (The "Reactive" Engine)
*   **What it is:** Ability to create, assign, and complete tickets from a phone.
*   **Why it's vital:** Eliminates the "black hole" of paper notes and radio calls. It ensures accountability and speed.
*   **The 80% Impact:** Drastically reduces response time to guest complaints.

### 2. Preventive Maintenance (PM) Scheduling (The "Proactive" Engine)
*   **What it is:** Automated recurring tasks (e.g., "Check HVAC filters monthly") linked to specific assets.
*   **Why it's vital:** Prevents the catastrophic failures that cost the most money and reputation.
*   **The 80% Impact:** Extends asset lifespan and prevents guest downtime.

### 3. Asset Registry & History (The "Data" Backbone)
*   **What it is:** A digital twin of every piece of equipment (Make, Model, Serial #, Install Date).
*   **Why it's vital:** You cannot maintain what you don't track. Knowing that "Room 202's AC broke 5 times this month" is actionable intelligence.
*   **The 80% Impact:** Informed "Repair vs. Replace" decisions.

### 4. Photo Evidence
*   **What it is:** Required "Before" and "After" photos for every task.
*   **Why it's vital:** Proof of work. It builds trust between the engineering team and management.
*   **The 80% Impact:** Accountability and quality assurance.
